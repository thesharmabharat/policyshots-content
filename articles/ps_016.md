## The big picture

A visual breakdown of revenue sources and spending heads in the federal budget —
where every rupee comes from, and where it goes.

## Where the money comes from

- **Taxes** — the largest chunk: income tax, GST, corporate tax, customs.
- **Borrowing** — the gap the government fills by issuing debt.
- **Non-tax revenue** — dividends, fees, spectrum auctions, and the like.

## Where it goes

- **Interest payments** on past borrowing — a surprisingly big slice.
- **Subsidies** — food, fertiliser, fuel.
- **Capital spending** — roads, railways, and other long-term assets.
- **Transfers to states** — their share of the pool.

## The deficit, in one line

When spending outruns revenue, the difference is the **fiscal deficit** — covered
by borrowing. Watching how it's spent (assets vs. day-to-day costs) tells you
more than the headline number alone.
