## Bonds, the quiet workhorse

Bonds rarely get the attention equities do, but they quietly fund everything
from highways to hospitals. This longer read walks through how they work and why
they matter even if you never buy one directly.

## What a bond actually is

A bond is a loan you give to a government or company. In return they pay you
**interest** (the "coupon") and return your money at the end (the "maturity").

- **Government bonds** are considered the safest — the state rarely defaults.
- **Corporate bonds** pay more, because they carry more risk.

## Why yields matter to you

When bond yields move, so does the cost of borrowing across the economy —
including your home loan. That's why markets watch them so closely.

### The retail angle

For years, bonds were mostly an institutional game. That's slowly changing, with
new platforms letting retail investors buy in with smaller amounts. It won't
replace your equity SIP, but it's a useful counterweight when markets get rough.
